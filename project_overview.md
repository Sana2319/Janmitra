# 📌 Problem Statement

## Community and Civic Participation — For Those Excluded from Decision-Making Spaces

Despite the Gram Sabha being one of the most important pillars of local self-governance in India, meaningful citizen participation remains limited. A large section of society—particularly rural residents, women, youth, senior citizens, and marginalized communities—often remain excluded from local decision-making.

Several challenges contribute to this problem:

- Citizens are unable to understand lengthy government agenda documents because they are written in complex administrative language.
- Many villagers are unaware of Gram Sabha discussions before meetings.
- There is no easy platform for citizens to submit concerns or participate digitally.
- Panchayat officials receive complaints from multiple sources, making prioritization difficult.
- Budget allocation often lacks transparency and may not accurately reflect community priorities.
- Young citizens have limited awareness of how local governance, public budgeting, RTI, and civic participation actually work.

As a result, important community issues remain unresolved, public participation decreases, and trust between citizens and local authorities gradually weakens.

---

# 💡 Our Solution

## JanMitra

JanMitra is an AI-powered civic participation platform that bridges the communication gap between citizens and local governments.

Instead of relying solely on traditional governance processes, JanMitra enables citizens to actively participate in decision-making through intelligent digital tools powered by Artificial Intelligence.

The platform converts citizen voices into structured governance insights that help Panchayat officials make faster, smarter, and more transparent decisions.

---

# 🎯 Objectives

JanMitra aims to:

# 🚀 Key Features

## 🤖 AI Complaint Analysis

Citizens can digitally submit complaints using a simple interface.

Gemini AI automatically:

- Generates complaint summaries
- Detects complaint category
- Predicts urgency level
- Assigns responsible department
- Suggests corrective actions

This significantly reduces manual effort while improving complaint management.

---

## 📊 Community Pulse Dashboard

Every complaint contributes to a real-time analytics dashboard.

The Community Pulse identifies:

- Most discussed issues
- High, medium and low priority complaints
- Complaint distribution
- Community trends
- AI-generated governance insights

This helps administrators quickly identify emerging problems.

---

## 📋 AI Generated Gram Sabha Agenda

Instead of manually reviewing complaints, JanMitra automatically creates structured agenda items for upcoming Gram Sabha meetings.

Each agenda contains:

- Priority level
- Citizen reports
- AI recommendations
- Suggested departments

This ensures meetings focus on the issues that matter most.

---

## 💰 Participatory Budget Planner

JanMitra recommends budget allocation based on real citizen complaints.

Rather than relying solely on manual planning, the platform estimates how village development funds should be distributed according to community priorities.

This promotes evidence-based governance and transparent public spending.

---

## 🏛 Government Dashboard

The Government Dashboard allows administrators to:

- Monitor complaints
- Track issue status
- Update resolutions
- Generate AI Governance Reports
- Monitor village health indicators

This centralizes governance workflows in one place.

---

## 📑 AI Governance Report

Using Gemini AI, JanMitra generates detailed governance reports including:

- Executive Summary
- Top Community Issues
- Budget Recommendations
- Agenda Recommendations
- Village Health Score

The report provides administrators with actionable insights before Gram Sabha meetings.

---

## 🎮 Civic Leadership Simulator

One of JanMitra's unique features is the Decision Simulator.

Users become the village leader and manage situations such as:

- Water shortages
- Road maintenance
- Floods and emergencies
- Education
- Healthcare
- Budget allocation

Every decision affects:

- Citizen satisfaction
- Village health
- Remaining budget
- Governance score

This makes civic education engaging while teaching users how public administration works.
